Thus hausing finished Europe, we come vn- to the Isthmus or Straight-land, which is between the head of the River Tanais and the North sea, which is in deed the bound of Europe and Asia : from it we step into Asia. Which, after we have a while beheld, we left, and so passed into Afri- ca, by the Streights, or narrow piece of the Mainland, which runneth between the Bay of Arabia, and the gulfe of Damiata, (Sirbonus Lacus) made by the ouerflowing of the Midland-sea. From thence passing through Egypt and Barabry wee came vnto the Streights of Gibraltar, which we crossed, and at the length came home againe to our owne natiue countrey from whence we first set foote; like vnto a trauailer or a Pilgrime, which hath viewed and trauailed through many and sundry seuerall Nations and Countreys, pasing out of one into another, orderly as they lay and we situate one by another, ouer- skipping none, at last returneth lasely and ioyfully from whence he first set foorth. Thus farre of the Mappes themselues: Now let vs speake a word or two of the backside of the same. Because we thought it would be a thing nothing pleasing to the Reader or Beholder, to see the backsides of the leaues altogether bare and empty; we determined there to make a certaine briefe and short declaration and Historical discourse of euery Mappe, in the same manner and order as we said we obserued in the Mappes themselues; omitting nor concealing any mans name, that we had oc- casion to vse. Moreouer to these also we have added a Table of the names of all the Authors, that euer wee knew or had; out of which, those that are so disposed, may fetch a more ample and larger [discowse] and description of the seurall Countreys handled by them. Wherefore the students of Geography shall haue here, in the Authours thus named in order, and in the Catalogue of the Authours of the Geographicall Tables or Mappes, which we have set before this our worke, and lastly in these Tables themselues, a certaine shoppe, as it were, furnished with all kinde of instruments necessarily required in such like business : out of which, if peraduenture there may seeme any thing wanting, in his iudgement , either to be finishing of any Booke of that argument, or more fuller descriptions of any Countreys whatsoeuer, very easily, or in deed without any la- bour at all be may see, from whence it may be and by be fetched. These things they are which I thought good to admonish the Reader of. It remaineth now, that wee doe entreate every man, to take this our labour and entertaine it, with no other minde then it was by vs both begunne and finished, at the length set out and Imprinted. Farewell, and wish well to Francis Hogenberg, Ferdinand, and Ambrofe Arfen, by whose skil- full bands and extraordinary great paines and diligence almost all these Mappes were engrauen and cut.
