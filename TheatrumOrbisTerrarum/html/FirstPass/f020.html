But what is that to Europe this part of the world? One might easilier beleeue it to haue been so named of Europus, who, as Trogus Pompeius witnesseth, sometime in these parts possessed a large kingdome: which also I do see to be auouched by Eusta- thius vpon Lycophron, who maketh this Europis to be the sonne of one Himerus. Pausanias saith that one Eurpoa was king of Sicyonia, a prouince of Peloponnelius in Greece: to him Eusebius in his Chronicle doth ascribe, who makeuth him equall to the Patriarke Abraham, & to hauve liued aboue 3550. years since, about 1950. yeares before the birth of Christ. There be some, as Festus writeth, that thinke it so named of the beautifulnesse and excellency of the country.
